Early Life

Dr. A.P.J Abdul Kalam was born October 15, 1931 into a muslim family of Tamil origin in Rameshwaram, Tamil Nadu. His family was poor, so he needed to go to work to help his family financially. He was a bright student, especially in maths. He studied physics in university and later went on to study areospace engineering.

Career as a Scientist

Dr. Abdul Kalam has been invovled in many scientifc endeavour. He was the project director of India's first Satellite Launch Vehicle (SLV-III), which deployed the satellite Rohini. During the 1970-1990s he was the project leader of the Polar Satellite Launch Vehicle (PSLV) and SLV-III projects. He also directed 2 ballistic missile projects, from the technology of his succecssfull SLV program, project Devil and Valiant. He also played a major part in developing many missiles under the mission including Agni, an intermediate range ballistic missile and Prithvi, the tactical surface-to-surface missile.1998, along with cardiologist Soma Raju, Kalam developed a low cost coronary stent, named the "Kalam-Raju Stent". They also designed the Kalam-Raju Tablet", a rugged tablet computer for health care in rural areas.

Presidency

Dr. Abdul Kalam served as the 11th president of India, winning by a majority of the electoral vote. He was president from 25 July 2002 to 25 July 2007.

Post-presidency

After his term(s) as president, Dr. Abdul Kalam became a visting professor in many top Indian universities.

Death

During a lecture On 27 July 2015 at the Indian Institute of Management Shillong, collapsed 5 minitues into his lecture. He was rushed to a nearby hospital, but was confirmed dead at 7:45 p.m. IST. His body draped in the Indian flag was taken to his hometown of Rameshwaram. Over 350,000 people paid respects to him.
